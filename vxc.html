Virtualization V.S. Containerization

With Virtualization, we can run many OSs on a single physical server’s hardware resources, while with containerization, we can deploy multiple applications using a single OS but separate virtual machines or servers. Hence Virtual machines are great to support individual applications that garnt an OS’s full functionalities. When our main objective is to deploy multiple applications on a very same server, Containers might be proven more efficient because our usual priority is to reduce the number of servers.
As a matter of face, our Use case is also a vital factor. Containers are more efficient for takss with a short life spawns. With their quick set-up time, they’re ideal for tasks requiring just a few hours. Having a comparitively longer life cycle, VMs are more suited for tasks with a longer period of times.
Listed below are afew benifits of using Containers over visualization:
  • As compared to VMs, they consume smaller amount of space on the servers leading to take just a few seconds to load up!
  • They’re highly elastic, meaning providing such huge amount of resources are really not necessary. Hence, they can exploit resources from a serverin a more efficient and a dynamic manner making additional space free to be used by other containers or simply be retired.
  • It’s comparitively highly dense, which basically means that it enables much more number of objects to be run on a single physical server at a same time. In such a dense environment, host’s resources are fully utilized but not over exploited. Compaired to traditional virtualization, they allow much denser environments due to the fact that— They don’t need to host their own OS.
  • In such a situation of having a high resource pressure, the individual performance of each of the app is way better with containers as compared with the hypervisors. It is because, with traditional virtualization, the guest OS must also meet it’s own memory requirements, taking part of Ram away from the host.
  • With the help of just 1 OS kernel, there’s no necessity of having updates or patches over and over again to take an effect on all the containers. This way, mantaining and performing operations on servers becomes more efficient.
